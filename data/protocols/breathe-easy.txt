NIHR Protocol: BREATHE-EASY (NIHR-211902)
Triple Therapy in Moderate COPD — Phase III

--- PAGE 1 ---
1. STUDY SYNOPSIS
Study Title: BREATHE-EASY — A 52-week randomised trial comparing single-inhaler triple therapy (ICS/LABA/LAMA) versus dual bronchodilator therapy in moderate-to-severe COPD.
Sponsor: NIHR Respiratory Translational Research Collaboration
Primary Objective: To evaluate annual moderate-to-severe exacerbation rate with triple therapy versus LABA/LAMA dual therapy.

--- PAGE 2 ---
2. STUDY POPULATION
Target enrolment: 900 participants across 22 UK respiratory clinics.
Population: Adults with moderate-to-severe COPD and history of at least one exacerbation in the prior 12 months.

--- PAGE 3 ---
3. INCLUSION CRITERIA
Participants must meet ALL of the following:
- Age 40 to 80 years.
- Post-bronchodilator FEV1 30% to 70% of predicted.
- Post-bronchodilator FEV1/FVC ratio <0.70 confirming airflow obstruction.
- COPD Assessment Test (CAT) score ≥10.
- At least one moderate or severe COPD exacerbation in the 12 months prior to screening.
- Current or former smoker with ≥10 pack-year history.
- Able to use a dry powder inhaler device correctly after training.
- Written informed consent.

--- PAGE 4 ---
4. EXCLUSION CRITERIA
Participants meeting ANY of the following are excluded:
- Asthma as primary diagnosis or asthma-COPD overlap with predominant reversible airflow obstruction (>12% and >200 mL FEV1 reversibility).
- Long-term oxygen therapy >15 hours per day.
- Diagnosis of α-1 antitrypsin deficiency as primary lung disease.
- Active pulmonary tuberculosis or untreated non-tuberculous mycobacterial infection.
- Oral corticosteroid use >10 mg prednisolone daily equivalent for >14 consecutive days within 4 weeks of screening.
- Initiation of pulmonary rehabilitation within 6 weeks of randomisation.
- Known hypersensitivity to inhaled corticosteroids, beta-agonists, or anticholinergics.
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding.
- eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m².

--- PAGE 5 ---
5. RENAL AND METABOLIC EXCLUSIONS
Additional renal exclusions:
- Severe renal impairment (eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m²) is an exclusion criterion.
- Participants with eGFR 30–59 mL/min/1.73m² may enrol but require 3-monthly renal monitoring due to potential ICS-related effects on adrenal axis in severe CKD (investigator discretion).

--- PAGE 6 ---
6. ENDPOINTS
Primary Endpoint: Annual rate of moderate-to-severe COPD exacerbations over 52 weeks.
Secondary Endpoints:
- Time to first moderate-to-severe exacerbation.
- Change in St George's Respiratory Questionnaire (SGRQ) total score.
- Change in post-bronchodilator FEV1.
- All-cause mortality at 52 weeks.
- Incidence of pneumonia (safety endpoint).

--- PAGE 7 ---
7. PROHIBITED THERAPIES
Prohibited during the study:
- Additional inhaled corticosteroids outside the assigned regimen.
- Long-acting muscarinic antagonists or long-acting beta-agonists not provided by the study.
- Roflumilast initiation.
- Maintenance oral corticosteroids unless pre-existing stable dose documented at screening.
