NIHR Protocol: IMMUNO-BREAST (NIHR-207118)
Checkpoint Inhibitor Adjuvant Therapy in Early TNBC — Phase II

--- PAGE 1 ---
1. STUDY SYNOPSIS
Study Title: IMMUNO-BREAST — A phase II study of adjuvant pembrolizumab versus observation following standard chemotherapy in early-stage triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC).
Sponsor: NIHR Manchester Cancer Research Centre
Primary Objective: To assess invasive disease-free survival (iDFS) at 3 years with adjuvant pembrolizumab versus observation.

--- PAGE 2 ---
2. STUDY POPULATION
Target enrolment: 320 participants at 14 UK oncology centres.
Population: Adults with stage II–III triple-negative breast cancer who have completed neoadjuvant or adjuvant chemotherapy with residual disease or high-risk pathological features.

--- PAGE 3 ---
3. INCLUSION CRITERIA
Participants must meet ALL of the following:
- Age 18 years or older.
- Histologically confirmed invasive triple-negative breast cancer (ER <1%, PR <1%, HER2 negative by IHC/ISH).
- Stage IIA–IIIC per AJCC 8th edition.
- Completion of standard (neo)adjuvant chemotherapy within 12 weeks prior to randomisation.
- Residual invasive disease after neoadjuvant therapy (if applicable) OR node-positive disease after adjuvant therapy.
- ECOG performance status 0 or 1.
- Adequate organ function:
  - ANC ≥1.5×10⁹/L, platelets ≥100×10⁹/L, haemoglobin ≥90 g/L.
  - Total bilirubin ≤1.5× ULN (≤3× ULN if Gilbert syndrome).
  - AST/ALT ≤2.5× ULN.
  - Creatinine clearance ≥50 mL/min (Cockcroft-Gault).
- Written informed consent.

--- PAGE 4 ---
4. EXCLUSION CRITERIA
Participants meeting ANY of the following are excluded:
- Active autoimmune disease requiring systemic immunosuppression within 2 years (exceptions: vitiligo, controlled hypothyroidism, type 1 diabetes on stable insulin).
- Prior treatment with anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, or anti-CTLA-4 agents.
- Active interstitial lung disease or pneumonitis.
- Live vaccine within 30 days of randomisation.
- Uncontrolled HIV, active hepatitis B (HBsAg positive), or hepatitis C with detectable viral load.
- Pregnancy, breastfeeding, or unwillingness to use effective contraception during treatment and for 120 days after last dose.
- Concurrent malignancy except adequately treated basal cell carcinoma or cervical CIS.
- Systemic corticosteroids >10 mg prednisolone daily equivalent within 14 days of first dose (inhaled/topical steroids permitted).
- Organ transplant requiring immunosuppression.

--- PAGE 5 ---
5. STUDY TREATMENT
Experimental arm: Pembrolizumab 200 mg IV every 3 weeks for up to 17 cycles (approximately 1 year).
Control arm: Observation with standard oncological follow-up per local guidelines.

--- PAGE 6 ---
6. ENDPOINTS
Primary Endpoint: Invasive disease-free survival (iDFS) at 36 months.
Secondary Endpoints:
- Overall survival at 36 months.
- Distant disease-free survival.
- Safety and tolerability (immune-related adverse events).
- Patient-reported outcomes (EORTC QLQ-C30 and QLQ-BR23).

--- PAGE 7 ---
7. IMMUNE-RELATED MONITORING
Participants will be monitored for immune-related adverse events including colitis, hepatitis, pneumonitis, endocrinopathies, and dermatologic reactions per protocol-specific management algorithms. Corticosteroid taper guidelines are provided in Appendix C.
