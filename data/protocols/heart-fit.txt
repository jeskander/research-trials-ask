NIHR Protocol: HEART-FIT (NIHR-198734)
Supervised Exercise Rehabilitation in Heart Failure — Phase II

--- PAGE 1 ---
1. STUDY SYNOPSIS
Study Title: HEART-FIT — A randomised controlled trial of supervised exercise rehabilitation versus usual care in patients with chronic heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF).
Sponsor: NIHR Leeds Clinical Research Facility
Primary Objective: To assess whether a 12-week supervised exercise programme improves 6-minute walk distance at 12 weeks compared with usual care.

--- PAGE 2 ---
2. STUDY POPULATION
Target enrolment: 240 participants at 8 UK cardiology centres.
Population: Adults with stable chronic HFrEF on optimised guideline-directed medical therapy.

--- PAGE 3 ---
3. INCLUSION CRITERIA
Participants must meet ALL of the following:
- Age 18 years or older.
- Documented heart failure diagnosis for at least 3 months.
- Left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) ≤40% on echocardiography within 6 months.
- NYHA functional class II or III.
- On stable heart failure medications for at least 4 weeks (ACE inhibitor/ARB/ARNI, beta-blocker, MRA as tolerated).
- Able to walk independently with or without a walking aid.
- Written informed consent.

--- PAGE 4 ---
4. EXCLUSION CRITERIA
Participants meeting ANY of the following are excluded:
- Acute decompensated heart failure within 6 weeks of screening.
- Unstable angina or myocardial infarction within 3 months.
- Symptomatic hypotension (systolic BP <90 mmHg) at rest.
- Severe aortic stenosis or hypertrophic obstructive cardiomyopathy.
- eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m².
- Uncontrolled arrhythmia deemed unsafe for exercise by investigator.
- Primary pulmonary hypertension.
- Musculoskeletal or neurological condition precluding exercise participation.
- Enrolment in cardiac rehabilitation within the past 6 months.
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding.

--- PAGE 5 ---
5. INTERVENTION
Intervention arm: 12 weeks of supervised aerobic and resistance exercise, 3 sessions per week (36 sessions total), delivered by accredited cardiac rehabilitation physiotherapists.
Control arm: Usual care with written physical activity advice per NICE NG106.

--- PAGE 6 ---
6. ENDPOINTS
Primary Endpoint: Change in 6-minute walk distance from baseline to 12 weeks.
Secondary Endpoints:
- Change in Kansas City Cardiomyopathy Questionnaire (KCCQ) score.
- Change in NT-proBNP at 12 and 24 weeks.
- All-cause hospitalisation at 24 weeks.
- Safety: serious adverse events and exercise-related adverse events.

--- PAGE 7 ---
7. RENAL FUNCTION MONITORING
Renal function (creatinine, eGFR) will be checked at baseline and week 12. Participants with eGFR 30–44 mL/min/1.73m² may be enrolled at investigator discretion with enhanced monitoring.
