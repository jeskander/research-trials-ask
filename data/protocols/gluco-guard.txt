NIHR Protocol: GLUCO-GUARD (NIHR-204521)
Metformin for Prediabetes Prevention — Phase III

--- PAGE 1 ---
1. STUDY SYNOPSIS
Study Title: GLUCO-GUARD — A randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial of metformin extended-release for prevention of type 2 diabetes in adults with prediabetes.
Sponsor: NIHR Cambridge Biomedical Research Centre
Primary Objective: To determine whether metformin ER 1500 mg daily reduces progression to type 2 diabetes over 3 years compared with placebo.

--- PAGE 2 ---
2. STUDY POPULATION
Target enrolment: 1,200 participants across 18 UK sites.
Population: Adults aged 30–70 years with biochemically confirmed prediabetes.

--- PAGE 3 ---
3. INCLUSION CRITERIA
Participants must meet ALL of the following:
- Age 30 to 70 years at screening (inclusive).
- HbA1c 6.0% to 6.4% (42–47 mmol/mol) on two occasions at least 2 weeks apart.
- Fasting plasma glucose 5.6 to 6.9 mmol/L.
- BMI 25.0 to 45.0 kg/m².
- Willing and able to provide written informed consent.
- Able to attend scheduled follow-up visits for 36 months.

--- PAGE 4 ---
4. EXCLUSION CRITERIA
Participants meeting ANY of the following are excluded:
- Established type 2 diabetes (HbA1c ≥6.5% or fasting glucose ≥7.0 mmol/L).
- eGFR <45 mL/min/1.73m² (CKD-EPI equation).
- Active liver disease or ALT/AST >2.5× upper limit of normal.
- Current use of metformin, GLP-1 receptor agonists, SGLT2 inhibitors, or insulin.
- History of lactic acidosis.
- Pregnancy, breastfeeding, or planning pregnancy during the study period.
- Known hypersensitivity to metformin.
- Participation in another interventional clinical trial within 30 days.

--- PAGE 5 ---
5. PROHIBITED MEDICATIONS
The following medications are prohibited during the study unless medically necessary and approved by the medical monitor:
- Metformin (any formulation).
- Sulfonylureas initiated for glycaemic control.
- GLP-1 receptor agonists.
- SGLT2 inhibitors.
- Thiazolidinediones.
Short courses of systemic corticosteroids require documentation in the case report form.

--- PAGE 6 ---
6. ENDPOINTS
Primary Endpoint: Time to diagnosis of type 2 diabetes (HbA1c ≥6.5% on two consecutive occasions or fasting glucose ≥7.0 mmol/L) at 36 months.
Secondary Endpoints:
- Change in HbA1c from baseline to 12, 24, and 36 months.
- Change in body weight and waist circumference.
- Incidence of metabolic syndrome.
- Safety and tolerability of metformin ER.

--- PAGE 7 ---
7. STUDY DESIGN
Randomisation 1:1 to metformin ER 1500 mg once daily or matched placebo.
Treatment duration: 36 months with visits at months 1, 3, 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, and 36.
